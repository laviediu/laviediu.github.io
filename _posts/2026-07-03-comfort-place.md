It is drizzling in Jeju and it will stay like this during my visit. I thought it would be horrible, but I guess something beautiful keeps shining its beauty in any weather. It is not too hot, which is better than scorching sun. Although I probably won't see a beautiful sunset, the fog offers a different kind of comfort.<br>
<br>
I am staying at different places every night, today I am in downtown. The room is very nice, it is too big for myself. Like the delicious king-size mackerel and abalone seaweed soup that I ate for lunch, I couldn't finish it. I have always found this quiet self companionship to be enough, but it has been different since that day. I still feel contentment though. I was laughing at sea cockroaches (they exist?!), I hiked for a bit and saw mesmerizing view of Hamdeok beach, there were a lot of pretty flowers on the trail (I am always excited to see them!!).<br>
<br>
I am grateful that I have the opportunity to witness these beautiful creations. And yet, I wish there was a smile and an adorable complaint. A smile when I gave up two third of the unbelievable Korean food portions. An adorable complaint when I walked too much. I guess God is teaching me how much companionship matters.<br>
<br>
This is my solo travel after more than a year. I did the last one when I explored Singapore. Since Seoul is boring for me, I decided to visit Jeju. I planned a very flexible itinerary since the weather might not be travel-friendly. I booked an ocean view room for tomorrow, I can't wait to stroll along the coast in the evening. I think I just found a new comfort place beside Kasai Rinkai Park. Jeju is very serene and the island is quite huge, I wish I can go to a lot of remote beaches next time. I think it's also the fact that I have been consuming Korean culture for almost ten years now. I met very kind strangers on the street, there was a kind haelmoni who helped me navigate Seoul subway, a kind man who helped me with my huge luggage on the stair, a kind couple who showed me where to look for an elevator in the Seoul subway, a kind woman who told me that my T-Money dropped on the subway.<br>
<br>
The public transportation is much cheaper than Japan, although the food cost is similar. I think I used some of my salary from the part time job well. Mom texted, “Be careful and enjoy your time there, you should always be happy”. I laughed and said to her, “Of course I can't always be happy, that's not life. Happiness will feel like happiness if I'm not always happy”, unintentionally philosophizing her. Perhaps I should bring her to Karimunjawa next September when I am home.<br>
<br>
I think I should continue my part time job in August before the contract ends. Oh I also found a new interesting internship company, I guess I will try to apply there. Somehow I like the direction of my life lately, I don't chase anything for winning, I try my best and I am grateful for the things Allah provided for me. If I count how many times I failed this year, probably it would be a dozen. There was the CERN summer school rejection, the University of Twente ghosting, the i-dont-know-why-i-applied internship rejections (too many), oh and lately the desk rejection from Acta Materialia. Yeah, it's a high target for a first paper. Mbak Afi said, “That's normal for a first paper, keep pushing it and come to Finland later, I will stay here after the graduation”. I guess I will submit it to Journal of Alloys and Compounds and pray for less revisions. Of course, I will save my salary to go see the Aurora in Finland too. I remember a student from Prof. Kim’s lab saying, “What if my master's application gets rejected”. I said to him, “Well, that's a process nonetheless”. These are all a process.<br>
<br>
Right, I was secretly writing to you. I still cry every now and then. My chest still feels heavy every now and then. However, I know that God loves us both, and it is enough for me to keep going. You should be with someone who brings you peace.

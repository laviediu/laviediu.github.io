I pulled an all-nighter yesterday, I have missed morning prayer time twice, oh summer. I did manage to finish my paper review presentation from 9 pm to 4 am. We just had a welcoming party for a visiting student in the lab. "Let's talk about what we talked last September. I have a hard time with this", he said during the party. I wonder why some of my male juniors often share their personal life to me, but I am grateful that I can be a safe space, an older sister I might say, for them.<br>
<br>
My schedule is very tight, yet my mind keeps giving space to this thing. I don't resent it anymore. I sang a lot recently, out loud. I don't know how I should redirect all of these emotions in my body, perhaps I should start jogging again.<br>
<br>
I talked to mom yesterday. To my surprise, she said that I need to settle before committing to do this. It is probably me, I said that I needed to settle first. Then, I change my words. It made me think somehow though.<br>
<br>
*"What does a woman bring to the table?"*<br>
<br>
With a man, what he brings to the table is physically seen. However, what a woman brings to the table is physically unobservable. I am not saying whether both a man and a woman are not capable otherwise, I am talking about the very reason how they are differently shaped. Here, I exclude the outliers. Sure, let's put evolution, a woman is expected to nurture, a man is expected to protect. It then comes down to biology, with women having an ability to reproduce. With men having much more stable hormonal changes.<br>
<br>
If a woman were to bring what a man does, yes, she is capable. If a man were to give what a woman brings, yes, he is surely able. Yet, would she feel fulfilled? Because I wouldn't. Would he feel fulfilled? I am not a man, I have no right to say their perspectives.<br>
<br>
*"So, what do I bring to the table?"*<br>

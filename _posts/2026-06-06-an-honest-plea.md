O Allah, how do I coexist with grief? You saw me, this morning, I woke up confused because my brain was subconsciouly processing the separation. My first thought when I woke up was, "he is not coming back, is he?", before I took a wudu for subuh. My chest was still heavy. And I sobbed too much in the lab.<br>
<br>
O Allah, how could I not love him? You see him, You know how he has lived his life. Alya knows how he led his teammates. O Allah, he is a good person, a really good person. This morning, through Al-Jumuah, while I laughed because it was the page I randomly opened in Friday, You said that You do not guide people who do wrong. He is doing the right thing, O Allah. You saw how he was letting me go, it must have been very tough for him too.<br>
<br>
Even if I am not someone he is looking for, with all the prayers I could send to You, I am pleading to You for his good deeds. O Allah, You are The Most Merciful, The Most Just, I am the exhibit. You see how kind he is to me. Even if my sins are as vast as the Earth's ocean, could I make a plea to You for him to meet his mother again? Even if I will not enter Jannah, could You let him enter that holy place?<br>
<br>
O Allah, who am I to judge? You know the best how You will give him Your Light. I know nothing. O Allah, I do not even have the right to judge how You will let someone enter Jannah. You let a prostitute enter Jannah just because the person was taking care of a dog. O Allah, who am I to judge myself when You know best what my every intention is, when You know how my heart is. As You said this morning through Al-Jumuah, You are The Holy One with the power to decide. What do I know about heaven and hell rulings. Looking at my sins, I would enter hell. O Allah, but at least let me try to live better.<br>
<br>
O Allah, I always ask You to never take this faith from my heart because I know how I will live my life without it. I know I am not Your best servant. I also have a lot of questions. I also wonder whether some people are delusional about faith. Yet, this religion is good for me, especially for someone like me. I believe in You because You show me the guidance.<br>
<br>
O Allah, You know that the plan was him or nothing. You know that he was supposed to be the last one *in my plan*. You know how I have been trying. You saw, four years ago, how I was holding myself back from calling him and telling him everything. You saw how I sobbed that day too. I know You have been training me to put everything in You because these are not the things a human being should carry, not for me, not for someone else. O Allah, even if I am not given a chance to meet another soul who recognizes my soul in this world, at least let me see that dream again. The dream where I was standing behind a huge waterfall with a very beautiful rainbow. If that dream is already very beautiful, how would a true Jannah look like?<br>
<br>
O Allah, just like how The Prophet was given ease with the insecurities The Prophet had during the first revelation, I am pleading to You to make it easy for him too. O Allah, just like how You took people The Prophet loved, one by one, when The Prophet was very young, soften his heart to accept You.<br>
<br>
O Allah, You are The Most Merciful, how do I make sense of this?

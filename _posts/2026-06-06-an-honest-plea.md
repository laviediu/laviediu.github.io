O Allah, how do I coexist with grief? You saw me, this morning, I woke up confused because my brain was subconsciouly processing the separation. My first thought when I woke up was, "he is not coming back, is he?", before I took a wudu for subuh. My chest was still heavy. And I sobbed too much in the lab.<br>
<br>
O Allah, how could I not love him? You see him, You know how he has lived his life. O Allah, he is a good person, a really good person. This morning, through Al-Jumuah, while I laughed because it was the page I randomly opened in Friday, You said that You do not guide people who do wrong. He is doing the right thing, O Allah. You saw how he was letting me go, it must have been very tough for him too.<br>
<br>
Even if I am not someone he is looking for, with all the prayers I could send to You, I am pleading to You for his good deeds. O Allah, You are The Most Merciful, The Most Just, I am the exhibit. You see how kind he is to me. Even if my sins are as vast as the Earth's ocean, could I make a plea to You for him to meet his mother again? Even if I will not enter Jannah, could You let him enter that holy place?<br>
<br>
O Allah, just like how The Prophet was given ease with the insecurities The Prophet had during the first revelation, I am pleading to You to make it easy for him too. O Allah, just like how You took people The Prophet loved, one by one, when The Prophet was very young, soften his heart to accept You.<br>
<br>
O Allah, You are The Most Merciful, how do I make sense of this?

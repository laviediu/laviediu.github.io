It is 6 am. I woke up for morning prayer at 2.30 am, then I was working on the presentation my colleague about to do today, reviewing my research visit document, checking my data analysis script, and obeying the daily Duolingo reminder. What a demanding bird. Ever since that day, my prayer hasn't changed. I ask God to guide you, if you're truly looking. And I ask Him to take you away from my life, if you're not good for me, my future, and my faith.<br>
<br>
I can't go back to sleep, my mind is flooded with all the things I'm about to receive and more things I've wanted to say.<br>
<br>
*"Whatever his answers are, at least I will have the clarity and be able to move forward. At the very least, when I look back to this moment 10 years from now, I won't regret anything"*, I soothed myself. I have been saying this for about a month since the letter, it keeps my sanity intact.<br>
<br>
If you're saying that we can't continue this, it wouldn't be a surprise, because honestly that's what I expect. Not just from you, but from anyone. I've never been convinced that someone could ever understand the things I hold on to. My history, the person I'm becoming, is not something someone can welcome lightly into their life. Not when the presence is inevitable.<br>
<br>
My faith is where I always return, it is a refuge. Without it, knowing myself well enough, I would be bitter, unbothered, lifeless, most of the time (*read: Hello, Cristina Yang!*). It is almost tragic that the very thing you see in me, perhaps the reason why you can't say no, originates from the faith itself.<br>
<br>
If you wrote that we should continue this, it won't stop here either. I don't really know you; your thoughts, your inner world, your expectations, your aspirations. I let you know about five percent of me, and I won't let the whole hundred of it to be known by anyone. If the faith thing felt like a test when it wasn't, this one would feel much more like that. Because it is. With the circumstances I was born into, stepping out of the castle I've built means risking myself to face horrible things I wouldn't see in my castle. I would ask uncomfortable questions, (over-)analyze, and occasionally spiral, but at least now I can ask myself to differentiate whether those horrible things are indeed dangerous or simply nothing. Welcoming me means allowing this person to learn, and allowing yourself to get hurt. And if you're not that person, then run.<br>
<br>
I don't believe in love without a binding contract, not because it isn't true, but because my brain can't process its accountability.

Hi, we finally found each other! Years ago, I wrote this poem for you.<br>
<br>
<br>
*gypsophila garden*<br>
<br>
*tingling slush of electricity in the skin*<br>
*thoughts racing through the crisp air*<br>
*one two three, a breath was taken away*<br>
<br>
*i walked through a garden of gypsophila*<br>
*would there be one white lily?*<br>
*perhaps the painter of flowers and leaves grew one*<br>
<br>
*the breezy wind caught my gown*<br>
*the calming sunlight bathed my cheeks*<br>
*i smiled, my hands were holding the white lily*<br>
<br>
*welcome.*<br>
<br>
<br>
I wonder what our ordinary days would look like. I hope they are not extraordinary at all. Let us fill them with discussing our work, laughing at political absurdities, deciding what to cook for dinner, watching good movies, reading books, doing living room karaoke, inviting friends over, having picnics at the park, planning our finances, dreaming about our next adventure, calling each other's family members, sharing future aspirations and dreams, understanding each other's struggles, celebrating each other's growth, respecting each other's quiet moments, as well as praying and understanding the Qur'an together. I hope our life is made of little things that quietly become the greatest blessings.<br>
<br>
Let us, years from now, expand each other's world. You will still have a world that is uniquely yours, and so will I. I do not want our marriage to replace those worlds, but to become the place from which we encourage each other to keep exploring them.<br>
<br>
We will misunderstand each other at times. We are two different people, raised by different families, carrying different histories, trying to build one home. Let us see misunderstandings as opportunities to know each other more deeply. Let us always remain curious about each other, because I think curiosity is one of the purest forms of love.<br>
<br>
Let us never become strangers to each other's inner world. No matter how many years pass, I hope we continue asking questions, listening with kindness, and making room for the thoughts that take longer to find their words.<br>
<br>
There will be seasons when one of us is stronger than the other. Some days I will lean on you, and some days you will lean on me. I hope we never become afraid of needing each other. We are not walking this journey alone. Allah entrusted us with each other so that we may help one another continue walking towards Him.<br>
<br>
The world will ask many things of us. It may ask us to be stronger than we feel, wiser than we are, more successful than is healthy, and more resilient than we think we can be. Sometimes it will make us believe that our worth depends on how much we accomplish, how little we complain, or how perfectly we carry our responsibilities.<br>
<br>
My love, let us leave those expectations at the door when we come home.<br>
<br>
If one day your responsibilities become heavy, tell me. If one day mine become too much to carry, I promise I will tell you too. You do not have to protect me from every burden, and I do not have to pretend that I can carry everything on my own. Let us remind each other that our worth has never been measured by how perfectly we perform, but by the sincerity with which we strive for Allah's pleasure. For whatever pleases Him will always be better for us than what merely pleases ourselves.<br>
<br>
I pray that Allah grants us children if that is what He has written for us. If He does, let them watch two imperfect people who continue choosing kindness, honesty, and mercy. Let us make them to never doubt that they are deeply loved. And I hope, long before they understand what love means, they will witness what it looks like.<br>
<br>
And if Allah does not grant us children, then I pray that our home will still be full of life, gratitude, laughter, books, long conversations, guests who leave feeling lighter than when they arrived, and countless reasons to thank Him.<br>
<br>
Most of all, I pray that our home becomes the safest place for both of us. Not because life outside will always be gentle, but because we choose gentleness when we walk through our front door. I hope that years from now, after the world has asked much from us, we will still look at each other and quietly think, "home".<br>
<br>
Thank you for choosing me every ordinary day, not only on the extraordinary ones. I promise I will keep choosing you too.<br>
<br>
Welcome home, my love.

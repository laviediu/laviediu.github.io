So, I continued listening to Steven's interview with John. I knew it, I knew this topic would come up, religious coping mechanism. I do not deny it either. I didn't fully realize the scale of my history shaping the way I think until recently. I have a lot of friends struggling with their own history. And I could see, very clearly, that a lot of them are using religion as a coping mechanism, they turn to God for refuge. Then I look at myself, huh I do exactly the same thing hahaha.<br>
<br>
However, this doesn't answer the question whether God exists or not. God certainly does not need *me* to worship Him, God does not need *me* to believe in Him, God does not need *all that*. *I* am the one needing *all that*. This reminds me of the ayah in surah Luqman I wrote in my 90 x 180 cm whiteboard, <br>
*"We gave wisdom to Luqmān, and said, “Be grateful to Allah;” and whoever is grateful is, in fact, grateful for his own benefit, and whoever is ungrateful, then Allah is free of all needs, worthy of all praise."*<br>
I love this ayah because it captures a very beautiful relationship between Luqman, a wise man, and his son.<br>
<br>
John brought the concept of faith in other religions, such as Islam. He put it as a *merit-based religion*, which is the exact opposite of Christianity. Now, I am not a theologian and I haven't read the Gospel, The Old and New Testaments, I don't even know if I wrote these Holy Book correctly and whether they refer to the same religion. However, if all believers, just by believing in Christ, have *the peace of mind* because they know that they will enter the paradise, then what is the point of life? If people like Jeffrey Epstein is now entering paradise, because he believed in Christ just before he passed, what happens with all the terrible things he did here on Earth? I *do not* have my peace of mind knowing *that*! And yet, *who am I* to judge what God will do to people, after all, I do not create this universe. After all, He is The Most Merciful, based on my religion. In fact, so much so, that for every single surah in my religion's scripture (except At-Tawbah) is begun with, <br>
*"In the Name of Allah, the Most Compassionate, Most Merciful"*<br>
Oof this is interesting, I should inquire why this exact surah is an exception. However, the salvation concept should not be this simple, just like if I say I believe in Allah without doing anything. What I find interesting is that, in my religion, when Allah says to believe, it is almost always followed by active actions, to believe *and do good deeds*. Huh, now I also find the concept of paradise level to be more compelling. I believe that God *should be* The Most Just. And since He is also The Most Merciful, there should be a justice in the Islamic salvation concept, just like how I graded the students' English essays.<br>
<br>
Let's go back to John's point. He also said, though, that we do not know what God will do, which is fair. *Who are we to dictate God's ruling?* Steven also asked about what would happen to the people before Christ? He answered that God does not punish people who do not have the knowledge. I agree with that. In my religion, the scripture is the last one sent as the perfect message from God. The scriptures before the last one also pointed towards the same God.<br>
<br>
Perhaps my biggest question to John is about Christ himself. I still can not comprehend why God *needs* to transform into a human form. In my religion, the explanation is neat, there are messengers sent by God for every civilization. In my religion's scripture, it is said that the exact numbers are only known by God. Several months ago, I was thinking about this concept. It is almost impossible, in the past, that the religion The Last Prophet brought could reach every single person on Earth. It also seems impossible that the message will be exactly the same, though if the message is the existence of one God and the teachings of doing morally good things, it shouldn't be *that* impossible. Hence, I really *do not* know. Yet, if He created the universe, this sort of things should be easy for God to handle.<br>

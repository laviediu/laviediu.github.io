I just came back from an experiment out of campus. I occasionally went to other university for measurements. It was finished at around 5 pm. My heart has been heavy, it has this itchy feelings that hurts. I am sleepy all the time, the visiting PhD student told me, "you look so sleepy", when I yawned every five minutes. My body has been asking me to get proper rest, and my hormones are roaring, I should be mentally and physically resting especially when I have my period. It has been so stressful with Seoul research visit preparation, new student mentoring, and catching up to synthesis before summer humidity ruins my samples.<br>
<br>
I justified my 1 hour night out at Kasai Rinkai park today, even though it is in the opposite direction from my house. I arrived when the sun already set. As expected, there was no one in my favorite corner. It is on the left side of the beach, behind the Tokyo Sea Life dome. On the far left side, I could see the bright lighting of Tokyo Disney Sea castle. On the far right side, I could see high rise buildings in the Tokyo metropolitan area. I sat on the rock pavement, with Cogon grass and vast sea in front of me. The sky was cloudy, the moon was playing hide-and-seek. I could see a lot of planes going back and forth from the Haneda airport. Presumably, the area is a tsunami-danger zone, which makes it even more perfect.<br>
<br>
I was looking at the beautiful moon, it was very bright that some parts of the sea reflect its brightness. I thought I would weep uncontrollably, but my tears were slowly shedding from the corner of my eyes. I was quietly asking God, when the moon reappeared from behind a huge white cloud, "when are you taking me?". I haven't been feeling this for a while. I didn't feel sad, I was genuinely asking. As if I was ready to go there, I am not.<br>
<br>
I feel guilty, for all the things I revealed. I should have kept them all from anyone. It has always been between me and Him. I keep hurting the person I love. I said I was afraid of breaking the person, yet I did. I threw grenades, out of the blue, to someone I cherish. Suddenly I was there, suddenly I came back, suddenly I exploded, suddenly it ended, suddenly everyone is hurt. I should have been firm with my principle, I should have shown what I typically show people, I should have continued my plan to never consider anyone. I should have done all that.<br>
<br>
Suddenly, there was a loud sound from the far left. It was a firework show from the Disney Sea. It was pretty, though I could clearly see the smoke poisoning the sky, "how did that pretty little thing affect the environment?". How did I make the person I love suffer?
